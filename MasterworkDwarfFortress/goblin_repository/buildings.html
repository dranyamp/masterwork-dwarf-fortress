Category Titles

All workshops are seperated into different categories in the build menu. These category titles can also be build, they will spell out their name. This can be used to label areas ingame, which is great for succession forts. They cost no buildmaterials.

A big amount of new reactions can be done here. You can extract new kinds of metal strands from metals bars, enabling silver, golden, mithril or orichalcum clothing. TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell and scales into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system.

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: Tan stomachs directly into a waterskins, rope from guts/intestines and pelts into a furs. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you dont want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for eariest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitinplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Chitin = Iron, very light.

Scaleplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Scale = Iron, fireproof.

Shellplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

Slaughterhouse -

Build labor

Beast Dissection
4 Blocks, 2 Mechanisms, 1 Saw Blade
5x3
Mason & Mechanic & Forge
building_slaughterhouse.txt

Every megabeast will leave a special corpse in this mod and can be butchered in the slaughterhouse to receive 3 items. A trophy that can be kept in a display case or traded away, a special reward that is specific to the creature, and a megabeast soul. The soul can be used in the Megabeast Kennels to train a single dwarf instantly to legendary in a chosen combat skill, or to revive the beast. Keep in mind that titans and forgotten beasts are not megabeasts and cant be affected by modding.

Megabeasts also give 60 meat, or 50 boulders, or 25 metal bars upon being butchered, depending on the bodytype of the beast. You will always get 1 heart.

Example: Kill dragon, butcher dragon corpse, get 10 straps of dragonscale. A steel quality, fireproof leather type. You will also get the dragon skull, worth a lot, and the soul.

The Pottery shapes and dries clay items. You can make clay, fire clay and kaolinite items from the collected raw materials, resulting in greenware items. Greenware is a special name for wet clay. The workshop will automatically queue two drying reactions, from greenware to leather-hard, from leather-hard to bone-dry. The items can not be used until they are fired in the Clay Oven.

To make it simpler: Shape Item => Dry => Dry => Fire Item.

The material progression is: Clay => Greenware => Leather-Hard => Bone-Dry => Ceramic

Each reaction has a 5% chance to fail, simulating impurities in the clay.

The ethereal forge can summon weapons and armors from the ether. These items are volatile and will disappear after a short while. You can summon axes, swords, spears, scythes, picks, bows, arrows and quivers, armors and siege ammo here.

Ethereal items are iron-grade and wither away after ~3-4 months.

Empyrean items are steel-grade and wither away after ~1 month.

Summoning high-quality items comes at a price. Warlocks are tied to their creation and if a masterwork item withers, they get bad thoughts. They might be more powerful in combat, but the mental sanity of the Warlock is the price you pay for it.

Graveyard -

Build labor
Skill
Buildmat
Dimension
 Requirements
Architect
Necromancy
4 Coffins, 4 Slabs
11x11
Mason or Carpenter
building_graveyard.txt
reaction_graveyard.txt

Scour the graveyard for items, old corpses, bones and lost souls. Maybe you even find a ghoul scavenging across the tombs, ready to be recruited into your dungeon.

Scouring for leftovers has a 3% chance of netting you a coffin, a totem or a slab.

Scouring for corpses has a 3% chance of spawning an "old corpse", which can be butchered for bones and a skull. You must view the "old corpse" and declare if for butchering manually.

Scouring for souls has a 3% chance of spawning a soul. It will wither away after a month, just like any other soul.

Attracting a zombie costs a stack of meat, and has a 25% chance of spawning a opposed-to-life zombie, which will randomly stumble around the map. They cause miasma underground, so make sure to do this on the surface. They hunt wildlife and attack invaders, but you cant control them.

Attracting a ghoul costs a stack of meat and a stack of bones, and has a 5% chance of spawning a new ghoul civ-member. It also has a 2% chance of spawning a feral ghoul, who will attack everything on sight. You have been warned.

The Torture Chamber allows two different reactions. You can torture prisoners, or you can torture your own Warlock.

If you torture a Warlock, the worker hast to be a Warlock. He will be injured and slowed down, but all other Warlocks in your dungeon will take it as a warning and work faster for one month.

If you want to torture a prisoner, you first need a prisoner. Bring any cage with a captured invader nearby. It has to be a sapient being, no animals or warbeasts. You can then corrupt your prisoner into a ghoul, which will join your dungeon.

Frost Giants and Automatons can not be corrupted.

Allows a more controlled form of necromancy.

Summon Soul Wisp creates a cheap and early scout, which can put enemies that kill it to sleep. They are great to spot and slow down ambushes and only cost a soul to make.

Pain Elementals are lost souls, angry souls. Using a soul and a gem, you create a Pain Elemental. They act like soul-wisps but violently explode and injure those around them.

Skeletons are the backbone of your fortress. They do labor and fight for you. One soul and a corpse, or a soul, 10 bones, a totem and some thread, are enough to raise a skeleton.

Zombies are animated corpses that shamble along the map. They are opposed to life and attack anything in range. Their bite is mildly poisonous. You need only a corpse to raise these, as they are soulless creatures.

 

Necromantic Altar -

Build labor
Skill
Buildmat
Dimension
 Requirements

4 Blocks
7x7

building_necro_altar.txt
reaction_necro_altar.txt

The epitome of necromancy, the Altar can revive megabeasts and even your own warlocks or skeletons.

Resurrecting a Warlock requires a Warlock to run the reaction. Your ghouls or skeletons are not versed enough in these spells. It costs 10 souls and can only be done once every three month per Warlock. This means you can raise 10 different Warlocks in the same week, but if one of them dies again, you have to wait 3 months. You can resurrect that same Warlock unlimited times, making them virtually immortal, as long as you can supply the needed souls.

Resurrection of skeletons is easier. You need 1 soul and can resurrect up to 3 skeletons that are nearby. They can be resurrected as often and as frequently as you like.

Reviving a megabeast requires a part of its body and its soul. Only megabeast souls are strong enough for this. You get both by killing a hostile megabeast and butchering its corpse in the slaughterhouse.

You may also use the megabeast soul for other spells, as seen in the Grand Convocation.

The vanilla kiln has been splitted into the kiln and the clay oven. The clay oven can make any items from clay, fireclay or kaolinite, as well as a unit of fuel. Clay produces stoneware items, Fireclay makes earthenware items and kaolinite produces porcelain items.

The "collect clay" zones have been renamed to "collect clay/dirt/peat", since more types of soil are valid targets now. Dirt can only be used as free boulders for constructions, while peat can be compressed and burned as low-cost fuel.

You can use plywood for one unit of charcoal per log, burn compact peat to 2-4 bars of coke and the very valuable anthracite for 12 bars of coke. Plywood is a byproduct of the wood-industry, compact peat can either be found in soil-layers or produced by compressing peat, and anthrazite is a rare coal cluster found deep underground.

You can create a metal anvil from five bars. You no longer need to bring one at embark.

Smelting ore also creates a waste product called Slag. These bars clutter up your smelter and should be destroyed in the slagpit or used up as buildmats.

Make an infernal trade with the devil. You have to sell a soul and you are asked for a wish. You can type the item name you want, followed by the material you want.

For example: pitchfork - enter - wood - enter. You will receive a wooden pitchfork. Or you try: great axe - enter - adamantine - enter. You will receive an adamantine great axe. The item can be anything, plant, weapon, armor, tool, and the material can be anything as well. Magma pot? Water Breastplate? Anything goes.

building_rose.txt
reaction_rose.txt

The Eternal Rose acts like a stasis chamber. You can produce them at the North-Western Library. Any Warlock that is pricked by its thorns will fall asleep on the workshop. They wont move, wont need food nor drink and wont wake up.

Any worker can come by and burn another eternal rose and wake all sleepers with its scent.

This means that you can wall in a handful of Warlocks and a Skeleton worker to wake them up, completely separated from the rest of the fort. While everyone else is dying from diseases, killed by dwarves or demons alike, your time capsule squad will survive and rebuild.
